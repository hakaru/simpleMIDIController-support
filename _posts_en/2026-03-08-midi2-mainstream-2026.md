# 2026: The Year MIDI 2.0 Goes Mainstream

2026 marks a turning point for MIDI 2.0. With OS-level native support, hardware manufacturers onboard, and DAW updates rolling out, MIDI 2.0 has finally become something you can actually use.

## What's Happening in the Industry

### Windows 11 Gets Native MIDI 2.0 Support

In February 2026, Microsoft released Windows MIDI Services, bringing native MIDI 2.0 endpoint and UMP (Universal MIDI Packet) protocol support to Windows 11 at the OS level.

Key features include:

- **Native MIDI 2.0 support**: UMP protocol at the OS level
- **Automatic MIDI 1.0/2.0 translation**: Backward compatibility with legacy devices
- **Multi-client access**: Multiple apps can access MIDI devices simultaneously
- **Loopback/app-to-app routing**: Virtual MIDI ports for inter-app communication
- **USB MIDI 2.0 class driver**: Plug-and-play USB MIDI 2.0 devices with no driver installation

macOS already supports MIDI 2.0 through CoreMIDI, and with Windows now on board, we have a true cross-platform MIDI 2.0 environment for the first time.

### The MIDI Association's 2026 Roadmap

The MIDI Association has laid out a 2026 roadmap accelerating the adoption of MIDI 2.0 Property Exchange. The roadmap also envisions integration with AI controllers and biometric inputs — laying the groundwork for next-generation musical expression.

### MIDI 2.0 Gear at CES 2026

CES 2026 saw a wave of new MIDI 2.0-compatible hardware announcements. Controllers, synthesizers, and audio interfaces across the board are making the transition to MIDI 2.0.

### The Evolution of Controllers

AI sensors working in tandem with MIDI 2.0 are enabling controllers that "predict the performer's intent." The combination of MIDI 2.0's 32-bit high-resolution control and AI-powered gesture recognition is creating entirely new possibilities for musical expression.
